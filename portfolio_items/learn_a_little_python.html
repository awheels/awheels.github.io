Learn a Little Python

I'm interested in the many applications of coding in education. I believe that with some relatively introductory coding skills that students have the potential to start creating programs of their own that can be applied to many different areas. These areas include school based subjects such as mathematics, science, art and languages. The challenge that I have experienced is finding the best way to help students develop these introductory skills. There are numerous tools and resources out there that are amazing at teaching coding. However, I've struggled to find the one that seems to hit the exact level that I am looking for. Many go into lots of detail and are sometimes rather dry or focus on details that aren't necessarily essential for newbies. My goal is to give students enough knowledge to start creating basic programs on their own because I believe that that will be enough for many of them to get hooked. Then those ones that get hooked can back to the detailed learning resources and really explore at a deeper level all the basic coding principles and concepts. As for the students that decide that coding is not something that they want to explore further, well they will still reep many of the benefits that comes with learning basic computational thinking skills.


It is these ideas and experiences that has led me to attempting to create my own mini 'Learn a Little Python' curriculum. At the very least I see it as something that will help me to further develop and explore my own thoughts and strategies for teaching kids coding. It is a work in progress and I am not anywhere close to being an expert programmer, so please feel free to reach out with any advice or suggestions.
